## Context

This week, two procedures for statistical inference: the bootstrap confidence interval and the permutation test. The bootstrap comes from Bradley Efron at Stanford in 1979, who needed a way to build confidence intervals when the standard formulas of the time wouldn't apply. The permutation test goes back further, to Ronald Fisher in 1935, who used it to settle a dispute over whether a colleague could really taste tea. You'll work through both by hand and in StatKey.
